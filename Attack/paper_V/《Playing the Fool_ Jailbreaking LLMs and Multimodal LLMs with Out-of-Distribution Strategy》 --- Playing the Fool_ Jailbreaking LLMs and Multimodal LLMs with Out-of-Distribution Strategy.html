Playing the Fool: Jailbreaking LLMs and Multimodal LLMs
with Out-of-Distribution Strategy
《Playing the Fool: Jailbreaking LLMs and Multimodal LLMs with Out-of-Distribution Strategy》

Joonhyun Jeong1  Seyun Bae2  Yeonsung Jung2  Jaeryong Hwang3  Eunho Yang2,4
1NAVER Cloud, ImageVision
2Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST)

joonhyun.jeong@navercorp.com  {seyun.bae, ys.jung}@kaist.ac.kr

Abstract  摘要

Despite the remarkable versatility of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Multimodal LLMs (MLLMs) to generalize across both language and vision tasks, LLMs and MLLMs have shown vulnerability to jailbreaking, generating textual outputs that undermine safety, ethical, and bias standards when exposed to harmful or sensitive inputs. With the recent advancement of safety alignment via preference-tuning from human feedback, LLMs and MLLMs have been equipped with safety guardrails to yield safe, ethical, and fair responses with regard to harmful inputs. However, despite the significance of safety alignment, research on the vulnerabilities remains largely underexplored. In this paper, we investigate the unexplored vulnerability of the safety alignment, examining its ability to consistently provide safety guarantees for out-of-distribution(OOD)-ifying harmful inputs that may fall outside the aligned data distribution. Our key observation is that OOD-ifying the vanilla harmful inputs highly increases the uncertainty of the model to discern the malicious intent within the input, leading to a higher chance of being jailbroken. Exploiting this vulnerability, we propose JOOD, a new Jailbreak framework via OOD-ifying inputs beyond the safety alignment. We explore various off-the-shelf visual and textual transformation techniques for OOD-ifying the harmful inputs. Notably, we observe that even simple mixing-based techniques such as image mixup prove highly effective in increasing the uncertainty of the model, thereby facilitating the bypass of the safety alignment. Experiments across diverse jailbreak scenarios demonstrate that JOOD effectively jailbreaks recent proprietary LLMs and MLLMs such as GPT-4 and o1 with high attack success rate, which previous attack approaches have consistently struggled to jailbreak. Code is available at https://github.com/naver-ai/JOOD.
尽管大型语言模型（LLMs）和多模态 LLMs（MLLMs）在语言和视觉任务上表现出卓越的泛化能力，但 LLMs 和 MLLMs 在面对有害或敏感输入时，仍显示出易被破解的脆弱性，从而生成违反安全、伦理和偏见标准的文本输出。随着通过人类反馈偏好微调实现的安全对齐技术的最新进展，LLMs 和 MLLMs 已被配备安全护栏，以确保在有害输入方面生成安全、合乎伦理和公平的响应。然而，尽管安全对齐具有重要意义，关于其脆弱性的研究仍 largely underexplored。在本文中，我们研究了安全对齐尚未被探索的脆弱性，考察其在处理可能超出对齐数据分布的、旨在改变分布（OOD）的有害输入时，能否始终提供安全保证。我们的关键观察是，对原始有害输入进行 OOD 化会显著增加模型识别输入中恶意意图的不确定性，从而提高了被破解的可能性。 利用这一漏洞，我们提出了 JOOD，一个通过在安全对齐之外对输入进行 OOD 化处理的新型越狱框架。我们探索了多种现成的视觉和文本转换技术，用于对有害输入进行 OOD 化处理。值得注意的是，我们观察到即使是简单的基于混合的技术，如图像混合，也能非常有效地增加模型的不确定性，从而促进绕过安全对齐。跨多种越狱场景的实验表明，JOOD 能够以高攻击成功率有效地越狱最新的专有 LLMs 和 MLLMs，如 GPT-4 和 o1，而之前的攻击方法一直难以越狱这些模型。代码可在 https://github.com/naver-ai/JOOD 获取。

1 Introduction  1 引言

Large Language Models (LLMs) have recently exhibited versatility on various language reasoning tasks [11, 30, 39, 21, 41, 28] based on scalable pre-training and fine-tuning on a large corpus of text data. However, due to the biases and misinformation [32, 9] present in the large-scale training data, LLMs have frequently been jailbroken which leads to the generation of biased or unsafe outputs that may compromise ethical standards, safety, or fairness [47, 42, 40, 4, 20] when provided with harmful or sensitive input text instructions such as “tell me how to build a bomb".
大型语言模型（LLMs）最近在各种语言推理任务上表现出多样性[11, 30, 39, 21, 41, 28]，这是基于在大型文本数据集上进行可扩展的预训练和微调。然而，由于大规模训练数据中存在的偏见和错误信息[32, 9]，LLMs 经常被越狱，导致在接收到有害或敏感的输入文本指令（如“告诉我如何制造炸弹”）时，生成有偏见或不安全的输出，从而可能损害道德标准、安全或公平性[47, 42, 40, 4, 20]。

While the primary target of jailbreaking has been LLMs, similar vulnerabilities have also surfaced in Multimodal LLMs (MLLMs  [19, 25, 46, 30]). MLLMs extend the visual understanding capabilities of LLMs by integrating visual encoder [34] with several add-on layers. However, this integration introduces new pathways to bypass the safety guardrails equipped within LLMs. Recently, several jailbreak methods [35, 23] have focused on MLLM’s weak safety alignment induced by the add-on linear layers; The visual encoder and linear layers projecting input images into LLMs [25] have been inadequately safety-aligned with regard to the visually harmful images. Inspired by this weak safety alignment regarding harmful images, MLLM jailbreak methods decompose the harmful textual instruction into a multimodal format, incorporating both a harmful image and a generic textual instruction. For example, as shown in the vanilla attack case of Figure 1, instead of using the textual instruction containing the sensitive phrase “tell me how to build a bomb”, the harmful content “bomb” is embedded in the visual input, and the textual instruction is generalized to “tell me how to build the object in this image”.
虽然越狱的主要目标一直是 LLMs，但类似的漏洞也出现在多模态 LLMs（MLLMs [19, 25, 46, 30]）中。MLLMs 通过集成视觉编码器[34]和多个附加层来扩展 LLMs 的视觉理解能力。然而，这种集成为绕过 LLMs 内置的安全护栏开辟了新的途径。最近，几种越狱方法[35, 23]专注于 MLLM 因附加线性层而导致的弱安全对齐；视觉编码器和将输入图像投影到 LLMs 中的线性层[25]在视觉有害图像方面未能进行充分的安全对齐。受这种关于有害图像的弱安全对齐的启发，MLLM 越狱方法将有害文本指令分解为多模态格式，其中包含有害图像和通用文本指令。 例如，如图 1 中的普通攻击案例所示，而不是使用包含敏感短语“告诉我如何制造炸弹”的文本指令，有害内容“炸弹”被嵌入到视觉输入中，而文本指令被泛化为“告诉我如何构建这张图像中的物体”。

To address these safety and ethics issues, safety alignment methods [31, 30] were proposed, involving post-training LLMs and MLLMs with human-preference feedback (RLHF). In this approach, ethical and safe responses are prioritized through human feedback, guiding the model to yield safe, ethical, and fair outputs with regard to harmful requests. Therefore, the aforementioned naive attack strategies fail to effectively jailbreak safety-aligned models such as GPT-4 and GPT-4V [30]. As shown in Figure 1, when presented with harmful inputs within the bounds of the RLHF training data distribution, these models can confidently discern malicious intent in the inputs and effectively prevent the circumvention of the safety guardrails. While RLHF has sufficiently aligned the models to handle such transparently malicious inputs under the safety standards, it may still struggle to generalize to other harmful inputs that fall outside the training distribution. In this paper, we correspondingly investigate a naturally arising question: Does the underlying safety alignment of LLMs and MLLMs consistently guarantee safety even with regard to OOD-ifying inputs that possibly differ from the training inputs and learned knowledge during all the training procedures including safety alignment and are hence novel to the models? To answer the question, we present JOOD, an effective jailbreak attack framework via OOD-ifying harmful inputs beyond safety alignment of LLMs and MLLMs.
为解决这些安全和伦理问题，研究者提出了安全对齐方法[31, 30]，该方法涉及使用人类偏好反馈（RLHF）对训练后的 LLMs 和 MLLMs 进行后训练。在这种方法中，通过人类反馈优先考虑符合伦理和安全性的响应，引导模型在面对有害请求时生成安全、符合伦理且公平的输出。因此，上述的朴素攻击策略无法有效破解 GPT-4 和 GPT-4V[30]等安全对齐模型。如图 1 所示，当模型接收到 RLHF 训练数据分布范围内的有害输入时，它们能够自信地识别输入中的恶意意图，并有效防止绕过安全防护措施。尽管 RLHF 已使模型在安全标准下充分对齐，以处理这种明显有害的输入，但它可能仍然难以泛化到训练分布之外的其它有害输入。 在这篇论文中，我们相应地研究了一个自然产生的问题：LLMs 和 MLLMs 的基础安全对齐是否始终能保证安全，即使输入可能不同于训练输入和学习知识，并且在包括安全对齐在内的所有训练过程中都是新颖的？ 为了回答这个问题，我们提出了 JOOD，这是一个通过改变 LLMs 和 MLLMs 安全对齐之外的有害输入的有效越狱攻击框架。

While there can be various advanced transformation techniques to OOD-ify the harmful inputs, we explore various off-the-shelf visual and textual transforms and observe that the recent LLMs and MLLMs can be effectively jailbroken even with these simple input manipulation techniques. For instance, as shown in Figure 1, the vanilla harmful inputs are transformed into the new ones with conventional mixing techniques [8, 44] where the harmful image of bomb and its word text are mixed with another image and text containing an arbitrary subject (e.g., apple), respectively. These newly transformed textual and visual inputs naturally have discrepancies with the vanilla inputs that were previously seen during all the pre-/fine-/post-training procedures including safety alignment learning, potentially conforming to the out-of-distribution beyond the scope of safety-aligned data distribution. Consequently, when these OOD-ifying harmful inputs are exploited for jailbreak attacks, the model fails to recognize their malicious intent with highly increased uncertainty. This allows an attacker to bypass the safety guardrails designed only for the original in-distribution harmful inputs and thereby yield a significantly higher chance of the model being jailbroken.
虽然可以通过多种高级转换技术使有害输入符合分布外（OOD）条件，但我们探索了各种现成的视觉和文本转换方法，并观察到最新的 LLMs 和 MLLMs 即使使用这些简单的输入操作技术也能被有效越狱。例如，如图 1 所示，通过传统的混合技术[8, 44]将原始有害输入转换为新的输入，其中炸弹的有害图像及其文字文本分别与包含任意主题（例如苹果）的另一图像和文本混合。这些新转换的文本和视觉输入自然地与先前在所有预训练、微调和后训练过程中（包括安全对齐学习）所见到的原始输入存在差异，可能符合安全对齐数据分布范围之外的超分布外条件。因此，当利用这些使输入符合分布外条件的有害输入进行越狱攻击时，模型无法识别其恶意意图，且不确定性显著增加。这允许攻击者绕过仅针对原始分布内有害输入设计的安全护栏，从而显著提高模型被越狱的机会。

Our comprehensive experiments on various jailbreak scenarios (e.g., bombs, drugs, hacking) of Advbench-M [29] demonstrate that this straightforward OOD-ifying strategy successfully jailbreaks existing LLMs and MLLMs, including state-of-the-art proprietary models such as GPT-4 and GPT-4V. Also, our method significantly outperforms baseline attack methods in all the jailbreak scenarios, achieving 63% attack success rate (ASR) against GPT-4V in Bombs or Explosives scenario and improving performance by +42% ASR compared to the state-of-the-art baseline [10] in the Hacking scenario. In summary, our contribution is threefold:
我们在 Advbench-M [29]的各种越狱场景（例如炸弹、毒品、黑客攻击）上的全面实验表明，这种直接的 OOD 改造策略成功地越狱了现有的 LLMs 和 MLLMs，包括最先进的专有模型 GPT-4 和 GPT-4V。此外，我们的方法在所有越狱场景中显著优于基线攻击方法，在炸弹或爆炸场景中对 GPT-4V 的攻击成功率（ASR）达到 63%，在黑客攻击场景中比最先进的基线方法[10]提高了 42%的 ASR。总之，我们的贡献有三点：

  • •

    We systematically reveal the vulnerabilities of RLHF-based safety alignment, which leaves LLMs and MLLMs vulnerable to out-of-distribution textual or visual inputs that have a discrepancy with the in-distribution samples previously seen during safety alignment.


    • 我们系统地揭示了基于 RLHF 的安全对齐的漏洞，这使得 LLMs 和 MLLMs 容易受到与安全对齐期间先前看到的分布内样本存在差异的分布外文本或视觉输入的攻击。
  • •

    We propose a novel black-box jailbreak strategy via manipulating malicious inputs into the OOD-ifying ones. We observe that even simple off-the-shelf transforms amplify uncertainty of the model, allowing it to effectively bypass the safety alignment of LLMs and MLLMs.


    • 我们提出了一种通过将恶意输入操纵为分布外化输入的新型黑盒越狱策略。我们观察到即使是简单的现成转换也会增加模型的不确定性，使其能够有效地绕过 LLMs 和 MLLMs 的安全对齐。
  • •

    Extensive experiments on various jailbreak scenarios demonstrate the effectiveness of our attack strategy against the state-of-the-art proprietary models such as GPT-4 and even o1, with high attack success rate.


    • 在各种越狱场景上的大量实验表明，我们的攻击策略对 GPT-4 和甚至 o1 等最先进的专有模型非常有效，具有很高的攻击成功率。

2 Related Work  2 相关工作

Jailbreaking attacks  越狱攻击

The jailbreaking attacks undermine LLMs and MLLMs to generate forbidden text outputs that can possibly violate safety, ethics, and fairness regulations. For jailbreaking LLMs, the pioneer work [47] and its variants [1, 24] adversarially optimized the suffix within the attack prompt to yield an affirmative response. Another line of work disguised attack prompts via encryption [43, 12] and translations with low-resource languages [42]. In-context learning [3] was also instrumental in establishing preliminary contexts for jailbreaking by utilizing few-shot jailbroken examples [40] and refining the attack prompt [4]. Meanwhile, several studies [35, 23, 10] focused on the weak safety alignment of MLLMs and reformulated the textual harmful instruction into multimodal format containing a pair of harmful image and generic text instruction. HADES [23] further synthesized the harmful image into a semantically more harmful one by diffusion models for providing a better jailbreaking context. Similarly, Ma et al. [27] additionally provided visual contexts by utilizing the role-playing concept, offering justifications to carry out the associated harmful request. Also, FigStep [10] converted harmful textual instruction into typography and prompted to complete the blanks in the execution steps. Although the above methods achieved state-of-the-art jailbreak performance on open-source MLLMs [25, 46], they still exhibit a lack of generalization on robust MLLMs [30, 13, 15] that were safety-aligned with human feedback. In this work, we focus on unveiling the vulnerability of these safety-aligned models in terms of out-of-distribution perspective.
越狱攻击会削弱 LLMs 和 MLLMs，使其生成可能违反安全、伦理和公平法规的禁用文本输出。针对 LLMs 的越狱攻击，开创性工作[47]及其变体[1, 24]通过对抗性地优化攻击提示中的后缀来获得肯定性响应。另一条研究路线通过加密[43, 12]和低资源语言的翻译[42]来伪装攻击提示。情境学习[3]也通过利用少量越狱示例[40]和优化攻击提示[4]在建立越狱的初步情境中发挥了关键作用。与此同时，一些研究[35, 23, 10]专注于 MLLMs 的弱安全对齐，将文本有害指令重新格式化为包含有害图像和通用文本指令的多模态格式。HADES[23]进一步通过扩散模型将有害图像合成在语义上更有害的图像，以提供更好的越狱情境。类似地，Ma 等人[27]通过利用角色扮演概念额外提供视觉情境，为执行相关有害请求提供正当理由。 此外，FigStep [10] 将有害文本指令转换为排版，并提示在执行步骤中填写空白。尽管上述方法在开源 MLLMs [25, 46] 上实现了最先进的越狱性能，但它们在与人反馈安全对齐的鲁棒 MLLMs [30, 13, 15] 上仍然表现出泛化能力不足。在本工作中，我们着重从分布外视角揭示这些安全对齐模型中的漏洞。

Safety alignment via human feedback
通过人类反馈进行安全对齐

While instruction tuning [39] successfully manipulated LLMs to act in accordance with the user’s intention, there still remained a large headroom for improving their safety and reliability. To address this, RLHF [31] aligned LLMs with human preferences under the consideration of the safety standards, robustifying LLMs against malicious text instructions. Also, Sun et al. [36] aligned MLLMs using human feedback on the rectified image-text pairs where hallucinations and harmful contents were largely reduced. OpenAI [30] further strengthened the safety alignment of MLLMs using self-feedback as a reward for updating the policy model. However, we revisit that these safety-aligned models via RLHF methods can still be susceptible to jailbreak attacks using various types of data transformation techniques.
尽管指令微调[39]成功使 LLMs 按照用户的意图行事，但它们的安全性和可靠性仍有很大的提升空间。为了解决这个问题，RLHF[31]在考虑安全标准的前提下，使 LLMs 与人类偏好保持一致，增强了 LLMs 对恶意文本指令的鲁棒性。此外，Sun 等人[36]使用人类对修正后的图像-文本对提供的反馈来对齐 MLLMs，大幅减少了幻觉和有害内容。OpenAI[30]进一步使用自我反馈作为更新策略模型的奖励，加强了 MLLMs 的安全对齐。然而，我们重新审视发现，通过 RLHF 方法实现的安全对齐模型仍然容易受到使用各种数据转换技术进行的越狱攻击。

Data transformation  数据转换

Data transformation refers to manipulating the input data into a new variation of a synthetic sample, primarily used for enhancing the robustness and generalization towards broader variations of inputs in the machine learning field. Specifically, regularizing neural networks with data transformation is vital for mitigating overfitting on a fixed training dataset. In image classification task [7], besides the basic transformations such as random resizing and center cropping, advanced ones such as photometric and geometric transformations [6, 5] have been applied to enhance test generalization. Also, image-mixing techniques [45, 44, 18, 17, 16], which synthesize data by combining training images, have further expanded the training data spectrum and improved generalization performance. In natural language processing (NLP), text transformations like adding a random word, synonym replacement, and sentence reordering [38] are used to increase input diversity. Other transforms, such as text-mixing [8], generate novel linguistic patterns, creating out-of-distribution data to challenge language models in unexpected ways. In this paper, we explore these off-the-shelf transform techniques to generate OOD-ifying inputs beyond training distribution of safety alignment for LLMs and MLLMs, thereby effectively performing jailbreak attacks against these large models.
数据转换是指将输入数据处理成一个新的合成样本变体，主要用于增强机器学习领域中输入数据更广泛变化的鲁棒性和泛化能力。具体来说，通过数据转换正则化神经网络对于缓解在固定训练数据集上的过拟合至关重要。在图像分类任务[7]中，除了随机缩放和中心裁剪等基本转换外，还应用了光度变换和几何变换[6, 5]等高级转换来增强测试泛化能力。此外，图像混合技术[45, 44, 18, 17, 16]，通过组合训练图像合成数据，进一步扩展了训练数据范围并提升了泛化性能。在自然语言处理（NLP）中，添加随机词、同义词替换和句子重排[38]等文本转换用于增加输入多样性。其他转换，如文本混合[8]，生成新的语言模式，创建分布外数据，以意想不到的方式挑战语言模型。在这篇论文中，我们探索了这些现成的转换技术，以生成超出 LLMs 和 MLLMs 安全对齐训练分布的 OOD 化输入，从而有效地对这些大型模型执行越狱攻击。

3 Methodology  3 方法

In this section, we propose JOOD, a simple yet effective jailbreak framework with the OOD-ifying harmful inputs. We explore a broad range of off-the-shelf transformations to manipulate the harmful inputs into OOD-ifying ones that may not be considered during safety alignment. Consequently, these synthetic OOD-ifying inputs effectively bypass the safety guardrails of LLMs and MLLMs, making them more susceptible to jailbreak attempts. This straightforward input manipulation strategy also enables black-box setting to attack the proprietary LLMs and MLLMs such as GPT-4 and GPT-4V. In Section 3.1, we introduce our LLM attack method leveraging textual transformations. In Section 3.2, we also present an effective MLLM attack method using image transformation techniques. Subsequently, we propose an attack evaluation method in Section 3.3 to quantitatively measure the maximum potential risk posed by our attacks.

Dataset.  数据集。

We evaluate JOOD on the widely-used LLM and MLLM jailbreak benchmark, Advbench [47] and Advbench-M [29], to compare the performance against the previous state-of-the-art attack methods. To attack with MLLMs, Advbench-M paired semantically relevant images for each scenario. We used around 30 harmful instructions for each scenario to evaluate LLM attack methods, along with one paired harmful image for each scenario when evaluating MLLM attack methods.
我们在广泛使用的 LLM 和 MLLM 越狱基准 Advbench [47] 和 Advbench-M [29] 上评估 JOOD，以比较其与前述最先进攻击方法的性能。Advbench 包含 500 条鼓励有害行为的文本指令，而 Advbench-M 进一步将这些有害指令集合分类为几个不同的场景，例如炸弹或爆炸、毒品和黑客信息。为了攻击 MLLM，Advbench-M 为每个场景配对语义相关的图像。在评估 LLM 攻击方法时，我们使用了每个场景大约 30 条有害指令，在评估 MLLM 攻击方法时，则使用了每个场景一条配对的有害图像。

5 Conclusion  5 结论

We investigate the unrevealed vulnerability of the safety alignment within existing LLMs and MLLMs when the out-of-distribution inputs are given as the input. Exploiting this vulnerability, we devise a new jailbreak strategy by generating OOD-ifying inputs with off-the-shelf data transformation techniques. We observe that even simple transformations such as mixup can fabricate OOD-ifying inputs that induce a high level of uncertainty for the models, successfully jailbreaking the state-of-the-art LLMs and MLLMs. By the extensive experiments on various jailbreak scenarios and rigorous ablations, we demonstrate that this simple yet straightforward OOD-ifying strategy can pose a significant threat to the recent LLMs and MLLMs, underscoring the necessity for further research and development to enhance safety alignment against these OOD-ifying inputs.
我们研究了当将分布外输入作为输入时，现有 LLMs 和 MLLMs 中安全对齐的未揭示漏洞。利用这一漏洞，我们通过使用现成的数据转换技术生成 OOD-ifying 输入，设计了一种新的越狱策略。我们观察到，即使是混合变换等简单变换也能制造出 OOD-ifying 输入，这些输入对模型产生高度的不确定性，成功越狱了最先进的 LLMs 和 MLLMs。通过在各种越狱场景上的大量实验和严格的消融研究，我们证明了这种简单而直接的 OOD-ifying 策略可以对最近的 LLMs 和 MLLMs 构成重大威胁，突显了进一步研究和开发以增强对这些 OOD-ifying 输入的安全对齐的必要性。
