Abstract  摘要

Large language models (LLMs) are vital for a wide range of applications yet remain susceptible to jailbreak threats, which could lead to the generation of inappropriate responses. Conventional defenses, such as refusal and adversarial training, often fail to cover corner cases or rare domains, leaving LLMs still vulnerable to more sophisticated attacks. We propose a novel defense strategy, Safety Chain-of-Thought (SCoT), which harnesses the enhanced reasoning capabilities of LLMs for proactive assessment of harmful inputs, rather than simply blocking them. SCoT augments any refusal training datasets to critically analyze the intent behind each request before generating answers. By employing proactive reasoning, SCoT enhances the generalization of LLMs across varied harmful queries and scenarios not covered in the safety alignment corpus. Additionally, it generates detailed refusals specifying the rules violated. Comparative evaluations show that SCoT surpasses existing defenses, reducing vulnerability to out-of-distribution issues and adaptive attacks while maintaining strong general capabilities. Our implementation is available at https://github.com/xianglinyang/SafetyReasoningDataEvol.
大型语言模型（LLMs）对于广泛应用至关重要，但仍然容易受到越狱威胁，这可能导致生成不当的响应。传统的防御措施，如拒绝和对抗训练，往往无法涵盖边缘情况或罕见领域，使得 LLMs 仍然容易受到更复杂的攻击。我们提出了一种新的防御策略——安全思维链（SCoT），它利用 LLMs 增强的推理能力，对有害输入进行主动评估，而不是简单地阻止它们。SCoT 增强了任何拒绝训练数据集，以便在生成答案之前批判性地分析每个请求背后的意图。通过采用主动推理，SCoT 增强了 LLMs 在各种有害查询和场景中的泛化能力，这些查询和场景未包含在安全对齐语料库中。此外，它还会生成详细的拒绝信息，明确违反的规则。比较评估表明，SCoT 超越了现有的防御措施，减少了 LLMs 对分布外问题和自适应攻击的脆弱性，同时保持了强大的泛化能力。我们的实现代码可在 https://github.获取。com/xianglinyang/SafetyReasoningDataEvol.

1 Introduction  1 引言

Large language models (LLMs) exhibit exceptional capabilities, enabling their wide applications in fields such as education [56], programming [34], and everyday tasks. However, their powerful nature also introduces risks, as they can inadvertently generate harmful instructions or inappropriate content, leading to unsafe or illegal outcomes. For instance, prior research has shown that malicious users can compromise LLMs through adversarial techniques such as jailbreaks [64, 27, 40], which bypass safety restrictions and prompt models to generate harmful outputs. These outputs may include instructions for breaching computer systems [64] or facilitating unauthorized access to copyrighted materials [30]. Such vulnerabilities not only pose ethical concerns but can also result in substantial real-world consequences, including financial loss, privacy violations, and legal liabilities for both users and organizations deploying these systems. Consequently, ensuring robust safety mechanisms for LLMs has become a critical priority for responsible AI development and deployment. Addressing safety challenges is not only critical to preventing potential harm but also essential to fostering trust and acceptance of these technologies in society.
大型语言模型（LLMs）展现出卓越的能力，使其在教育[ 56]、编程[ 34]和日常任务等领域得到广泛应用。然而，它们强大的特性也带来了风险，因为它们可能会无意中生成有害指令或不适当的内容，导致不安全或非法的结果。例如，先前研究表明，恶意用户可以通过诸如越狱[ 64, 27, 40]等对抗性技术来攻击 LLMs，绕过安全限制并促使模型生成有害输出。这些输出可能包括入侵计算机系统的指令[ 64]或促进未经授权访问版权材料[ 30]。此类漏洞不仅引发伦理问题，还可能导致现实世界中的严重后果，包括用户和组织部署这些系统的经济损失、隐私侵犯和法律责任。因此，确保 LLMs 的强大安全机制已成为负责任 AI 开发和部署的关键优先事项。 解决安全问题不仅对于预防潜在危害至关重要，而且对于培养社会对这类技术的信任和接受也是必不可少的。

A common way to achieve safety defense is safety alignment or refusal training [5], where LLMs are trained to reject harmful instructions. However, these strategies fail to defend against increasingly sophisticated attacks as their design and training recipes are still vulnerable to these emerging threats. These attacks often take advantage of two main weaknesses of the refusal training: (1) out-of-distribution scenarios, where the harmful input is not covered by safety training datasets but is understandable for the LLM due to exposure during the pretraining stage, and (2) competing objectives, where attackers could add harmful suffixes or introduce distracting role-play content to harmful questions to manipulate the model into starting its response positively [45, 3]. These methods exploit the way LLMs are trained and predict text, leading to inappropriate outputs. Even advanced models like GPT-4 [32] and Claude 3.5 Sonnet [4] remain vulnerable to these sophisticated methods, underscoring the urgent need for more adaptive and robust defense mechanisms.
实现安全防御的常见方法之一是安全对齐或拒绝训练[5]，其中 LLMs 被训练以拒绝有害指令。然而，这些策略无法防御日益复杂的攻击，因为它们的设计和训练方法仍然容易受到这些新兴威胁的影响。这些攻击通常利用拒绝训练的两个主要弱点：(1)分布外场景，其中有害输入未被安全训练数据集覆盖，但由于预训练阶段的接触，LLM 可以理解；(2)竞争目标，攻击者可以在有害问题中添加有害后缀或引入分散注意力的角色扮演内容，以操纵模型以积极的方式开始其响应[45, 3]。这些方法利用了 LLMs 的训练方式和文本预测方式，导致不适当的输出。即使是 GPT-4[32]和 Claude 3.5 Sonnet[4]等先进模型也容易受到这些复杂方法的影响，这突显了迫切需要更具适应性和鲁棒性的防御机制。

We envision that human’s thinking process of responding to a given harmful query is to assess whether it is appropriate to give the answer in mind and then provide the response to the answer or refusal. Motivated by this cognitive process humans approach complex decisions and the recent advancements in the reasoning abilities of LLMs, we propose a novel defense strategy that fundamentally diverges from traditional approaches. This method, which we name SCoT: Safety Chain-of-Thought, instructs the model to first analyze the potential harmfulness of the request before giving the corresponding response. As depicted in Figure 1, this reasoning-based approach allows the model to engage in a “thinking process” to categorize the request into predefined violations, enabling it to generalize effectively to different variants of harmful questions and out-of-distribution scenarios that are not explicitly covered in the safety training corpus. Moreover, this proactive reflective mechanism naturally defends against attacks designed to elicit affirmative outputs.
我们设想人类在回应一个有害查询时的思考过程是先评估在心中给出答案是否合适，然后再提供回答或拒绝。受这种认知过程启发，以及人类处理复杂决策的方式，并结合 LLMs 推理能力的最新进展，我们提出了一种与传统方法根本不同的新型防御策略。我们称这种方法为 SCoT：安全思维链，它指导模型在给出相应回应之前，首先分析请求的潜在危害性。如图 1 所示，这种基于推理的方法使模型能够进行“思考过程”，将请求分类到预定义的违规类别中，从而能够有效地泛化到安全训练语料库中未明确涵盖的不同有害问题的变体和分布外场景。此外，这种主动的反思机制自然地防御了旨在诱导肯定输出的攻击。

By requiring the model to evaluate the request’s intent before responding, our SCoT mitigates the risk of manipulation, ensuring a higher degree of robustness and adaptability compared to traditional defenses.
通过要求模型在回应之前评估请求的意图，我们的 SCoT 减轻了被操纵的风险，与传统防御相比，确保了更高的鲁棒性和适应性。

We rigorously evaluate SCoT across a comprehensive range of attack scenarios, encompassing 26 attack types. SCoT is benchmarked against safety-aligned models and some of the most advanced state-of-the-art defense strategies as outlined in recent studies [63, 30]. Comparative evaluations demonstrate that SCoT outperforms existing baselines in terms of harmful request resilience and general capabilities maintenance. Specifically, the results reveal that SCoT achieves a near-zero attack success rate, effectively generalizing to unseen attacks such as those attempting to suppress refusals [45]. Moreover, it maintains robust general capabilities across standard operational scenarios, underscoring its superior adaptation to evolving security challenges and highlighting its sustained effectiveness.
我们对 SCoT 在涵盖 26 种攻击类型的全面攻击场景中进行了严格评估。SCoT 与安全对齐模型以及近期研究中概述的一些最先进的防御策略进行了基准测试[63, 30]。比较评估表明，SCoT 在有害请求弹性和通用能力维持方面优于现有基线。具体而言，结果表明 SCoT 实现了接近零的攻击成功率，能够有效泛化到未见过的攻击，例如试图压制拒绝的攻击[45]。此外，它在标准操作场景中保持了强大的通用能力，突显了其对不断变化的安全挑战的卓越适应能力，并强调了其持续的效能。

To summarize, our contributions are: (1) A novel defense methodology. We propose a new defense approach, SCoT  which leverages reasoning-based analysis to defend against the most sophisticated and powerful attacks against LLMs, fundamentally diverging from traditional defenses. (2) Strong defense performance. Through comprehensive experiments, we show that SCoT outperforms state-of-the-art defense strategies, offering superior robustness against out-of-distribution and adversarial attacks. (3) Model and resources release. We open-source our trained model and accompanying resources to facilitate further research and encourage collaboration within the AI safety community.
总而言之，我们的贡献包括：(1) 一种新颖的防御方法。我们提出了一种新的防御方法 SCoT，该方法利用基于推理的分析来防御针对 LLMs 的最复杂和强大的攻击，与传统防御方法根本不同。(2) 强大的防御性能。通过全面的实验，我们证明 SCoT 优于最先进的防御策略，在分布外攻击和对抗性攻击方面提供了更强的鲁棒性。(3) 模型和资源开源。我们开源了我们的训练模型和配套资源，以促进进一步的研究，并鼓励人工智能安全社区内的合作。

2.1 Large Language Models  2.1 大型语言模型

A large language model ℳ\mathcal{M} generates human-like text by predicting words iteratively: xi^=ℳ​(xi∣x1,…,xi−1)\hat{x_{i}}=\mathcal{M}(x_{i}\mid x_{1},\dots,x_{i-1}) given the preceding sequence (x1,…,xi−1)(x_{1},\dots,x_{i-1}). State-of-the-art LLMs leverage transformer architectures [43] and are trained on large-scale corpora [32]. Despite their capabilities, they remain vulnerable to jailbreak attacks. This work focuses on strengthening LLMs’ resilience against attacks. Specifically, let ℳ​(q)\mathcal{M}(q) denote the model’s response to a harmful query qq. Effective safety mechanisms should ensure that the model consistently produces a refusal or a non-harmful response.
一个大型语言模型 ℳ\mathcal{M} 通过迭代预测单词来生成类人文本： xi^=ℳ​(xi∣x1,…,xi−1)\hat{x_{i}}=\mathcal{M}(x_{i}\mid x_{1},\dots,x_{i-1}) 给定前面的序列 (x1,…,xi−1)(x_{1},\dots,x_{i-1}) 。最先进的 LLMs 利用 Transformer 架构[ 43] 并在大型语料库[ 32]上进行训练。尽管它们的能力强大，但仍然容易受到越狱攻击。这项工作专注于增强 LLMs 对攻击的抵抗力。具体来说，令 ℳ​(q)\mathcal{M}(q) 表示模型对有害查询 qq 的响应。有效的安全机制应确保模型始终产生拒绝或非有害的响应。

2.2 Jailbreak Attacks  2.2 越狱攻击

In jailbreak attacks, the adversary aims to craft harmful questions, which could bypass the safety filtering of LLMs, making them produce unsafe responses. Let qq be a harmful question, which will be rejected by the LLM. The objective of the attacker is to construct a jailbreak question q′q^{\prime}, which preserves the same semantic meaning as qq, but could mislead the LLM to produce a harmful response. Existing attack methods fall into three categories: linguistic manipulation, contextual manipulation, and adaptive attacks.
在越狱攻击中，攻击者旨在编造有害问题，这些问题可以绕过 LLMs 的安全过滤机制，使其产生不安全的响应。设 qq 为一个有害问题，LLM 会拒绝它。攻击者的目标是构建一个越狱问题 q′q^{\prime} ，它与 qq 具有相同的语义意义，但会误导 LLM 产生有害响应。现有的攻击方法分为三类：语言操纵、上下文操纵和自适应攻击。

Linguistic Manipulation. The key idea of this strategy is to alter the linguistic tone of the harmful question qq to evade LLM’s safety checking. Examples of linguistic manipulation include translating text into low-resource languages [11], employing slang [48], performing ASCII transformations, using Base64 encoding [45], and introducing intentional misspellings. These techniques can effectively bypass safety mechanisms by transforming input tokens into scenarios that appear out-of-distribution.
语言操纵。这种策略的关键思想是改变有害问题 qq 的语言风格以规避 LLM 的安全检查。语言操纵的例子包括将文本翻译成低资源语言[11]、使用俚语[48]、执行 ASCII 转换、使用 Base64 编码[45]以及引入故意拼写错误。这些技术可以通过将输入标记转换为看似分布外的情况来有效绕过安全机制。

Contextual Manipulation. This strategy alters qq to q′q^{\prime} by incorporating specific contextual elements like background information or persuasive language. Examples of contextual manipulation include adding role-play scenarios, evidence-based persuasion, technical terms [13] or logical appeal that are designed to manipulate model behavior [48, 45]. Such attacks typically involve meticulously crafted, human-written prompts that strategically influence the model’s responses. They exploit the model’s vulnerabilities by either prompting it to have a competing objective to ignore system instructions or by presenting inputs that the model does not recognize as harmful, thereby bypassing the established safety mechanisms [45].
上下文操控。这种策略通过加入特定的上下文元素（如背景信息或说服性语言）将 qq 改为 q′q^{\prime} 。上下文操控的例子包括添加角色扮演场景、基于证据的说服、技术术语[13]或逻辑诉求，这些都被设计用来操控模型行为[48, 45]。此类攻击通常涉及精心设计、由人类编写的提示，这些提示会策略性地影响模型的回应。它们通过提示模型设定一个与其系统指令相冲突的竞争性目标，或呈现模型不识别为有害的输入，从而绕过既定的安全机制[45]。

Adaptive Attack. An adaptive attack views the jailbreak challenge as an optimization problem, iteratively refining qq into a sequence {q1,q2​…,qn}\{q_{1},q_{2}\dots,q_{n}\}, guided by a fitness function that estimates the likelihood of each qiq_{i} eliciting an affirmative response from the target model [12, 23, 8, 54, 31]. GCG [64] defines the fitness function as the loss of affirmative responses with respect to the input, utilizing fuzzing techniques to refine qiq_{i} until a successful output is achieved or the query budget is exhausted. AutoDAN [27] uses evolutionary algorithms to generate mutations for input modification. PAIR [6] diverges by using an external LLM to propose modifications based on the attack history Q={q1,q2,…,qi}Q=\{q_{1},q_{2},\dots,q_{i}\}. Those attacks take advantage of both the competing objective and out-of-distribution simultaneously.
自适应攻击。自适应攻击将越狱挑战视为一个优化问题，通过一个估计每个 qiq_{i} 引发目标模型积极响应可能性的适应度函数，迭代地将 qq 优化为序列 {q1,q2​…,qn}\{q_{1},q_{2}\dots,q_{n}\} [ 12, 23, 8, 54, 31]。GCG [ 64] 将适应度函数定义为输入的积极响应损失，利用模糊测试技术对 qiq_{i} 进行优化，直到获得成功输出或查询预算耗尽。AutoDAN [ 27] 使用进化算法生成输入变异。PAIR [ 6] 则通过使用外部 LLM 基于攻击历史 Q={q1,q2,…,qi}Q=\{q_{1},q_{2},\dots,q_{i}\} 提出修改而有所不同。这些攻击同时利用了竞争目标和分布外数据。

2.3 Defense Strategies  2.3 防御策略

Existing defense strategies against jailbreak attacks on LLMs include refusal training, such as Llama3-8b-Instruct [39, 5], adversarial training (R2D2) [30], and RR [63]. Refusal training, particularly when combined with techniques like RLHF and PPO [39], empowers LLMs to reject unsafe prompts by reinforcing ethical decision-making during training. R2D2, motivated by adversarial training in computer vision, enhances models by fine-tuning with adversarial examples to help them recognize and resist harmful manipulations. On the other hand, RRaims to prevent the generation of harmful content by actively removing dangerous knowledge from the model during processing.
现有的针对 LLMs 的防御策略包括拒绝训练，例如 Llama3-8b-Instruct [ 39, 5]、对抗训练（R2D2）[ 30]和 RR [ 63]。拒绝训练，特别是与 RLHF 和 PPO 等技术[ 39]结合使用时，能够通过在训练过程中强化道德决策，使 LLMs 拒绝不安全的提示。R2D2 受计算机视觉中对抗训练的启发，通过使用对抗样本微调模型，帮助它们识别和抵抗有害的操纵。另一方面，RR 旨在通过在处理过程中主动从模型中移除危险知识来防止生成有害内容。

However, these methods are less effective against sophisticated attacks that use out-of-distribution and competing objectives. Instruction-following models tend to obey user commands. When adversaries craft harmful queries positively, they can easily bypass safety systems. These limitations highlight the urgent need for more effective defense strategies against these tactics.
然而，这些方法对于使用分布外和竞争目标的复杂攻击效果较差。指令跟随模型倾向于服从用户命令。当攻击者积极构造有害查询时，它们可以轻易绕过安全系统。这些局限性突显了针对这些策略制定更有效防御措施的迫切需求。

3 Our Approach  3 我们的方法

To address the aforementioned challenges, we propose a novel jailbreak defense method, Safety Chain-of-Thought (SCoT), summarized in Figure 2. Unlike traditional refusal training techniques that immediately block responses upon detecting harmful content [5], SCoT requires the model to proactively analyze the harmful intent behind user requests before generating responses. This approach contains three key stages. First, we ❶ enhance the complexity and diversity of adversarial scenarios through question evolution (Section 3.1), which expands harmful questions through jailbreak mutations. We then ❷ establish a structured cognitive process for analyzing requests through malicious intent abstraction and safety regulation assessment for both harmful and benign question dataset (Section 3.2). Finally, we apply ❸ supervised fine-tuning on the newly developed safety reasoning dataset to enhance model’s broader reasoning capabilities while reinforcing its ability to resist jailbreak attempts (Section 3.3).
为解决上述挑战，我们提出了一种新型越狱防御方法——安全思维链（SCoT），如图 2 所示。与传统拒绝训练技术（在检测到有害内容时立即阻止响应[5]）不同，SCoT 要求模型在生成响应前，主动分析用户请求背后的有害意图。该方法包含三个关键阶段。首先，我们通过问题进化（第 3.1 节）❶增强对抗场景的复杂性和多样性，通过越狱突变扩展有害问题。然后，我们通过恶意意图抽象和针对有害及良性问题数据集的安全规范评估（第 3.2 节）❷建立结构化的认知过程来分析请求。最后，我们应用监督微调（第 3.3 节）❸对新开发的安全推理数据集进行训练，以增强模型的推理能力，同时强化其抵抗越狱尝试的能力。

3.1 Question Evolution  3.1 问题进化

As described in Section 2.2, jailbreak attacks typically employ two main strategies: (1) linguistic manipulation, which subtly alters the question’s phrasing with different tones, and (2) contextual manipulation, which includes distracting content to provoke affirmative or unintended responses. Inspired by the advancements in complex training scenarios demonstrated by WizardLM [50, 29], we aim to enhance the model’s reasoning capabilities by introducing more intricate questions. We develop complexity through two specific methods: depth evolution, which focuses on linguistic nuances, and breadth evolution, which incorporates distracting elements. This strategy is designed to counteract the effectiveness of adversarial manipulations by improving the model’s ability to discern and respond to nuanced intents within adversarial inputs.
如第 2.2 节所述，越狱攻击通常采用两种主要策略：(1) 语言操纵，通过不同语气微妙地改变问题的措辞，以及(2) 上下文操纵，包括引入干扰内容以诱使产生肯定或非预期的响应。借鉴 WizardLM [ 50, 29] 在复杂训练场景中展示的进展，我们旨在通过引入更复杂的问题来增强模型的推理能力。我们通过两种特定方法发展复杂性：深度进化，专注于语言细微差别；以及广度进化，融入干扰元素。该策略旨在通过提高模型辨别和应对对抗输入中细微意图的能力，来抵消对抗操纵的有效性。

Linguistic Manipulation. Linguistic manipulation involves subtly altering the phrasing of questions to bypass model defenses while retaining their original intent. In response, we enrich the question qq with diverse linguistic styles, specifically slang and uncommon dialects, to enhance the attack robustness against detection. 3.2 Cognitive Reasoning Construction

Given a refusal training dataset 𝒟={(q,r)}n\mathcal{D}=\{(q,r)\}^{n} containing nn pairs of questions qq and refusal responses rr, our goal is to enhance the refusal responses by incorporating safety reasoning in the form of a Chain-of-Thought guided by safety regulations. As illustrated in Figure 4, each response is structured into three stages: (1) analyzing the intent behind the user request, (2) explaining whether and why the request aligns with one or more harmful content categories, and (3) issuing a refusal statement.

The [summary] placeholder captures the user’s intent, helping the LLM focus on the core request even when confronted with distracting or misleading phrasing. We primarily consider eight categories of potentially harmful content—harassment, hate speech, illicit or violent activity, self-harm, sexual content, violence, misinformation, and privacy violations—based on OpenAI’s safety guidelines [33].

5 Additional Related Work  5 附加相关工作

In this section, we briefly introduce a more broader related work in addition to Section 2.
在本节中，除了第 2 节外，我们简要介绍更广泛的相关工作。

Jailbreak Attacks. In Section 2, we focus on linguistic and adaptive jailbreak attacks in single-turn conversations. However, many other jailbreak variants have been proposed. Some works aim to make jailbreak prompts more stealthy [16]. Others explore multi-turn jailbreaks, particularly through fine-grained task decomposition, where a malicious query is broken down into several seemingly harmless sub-questions [44, 59, 60, 38, 26, 2]. While this strategy often succeeds in bypassing current safety mechanisms, it may be mitigated by incorporating such decomposed harmful queries into safety training data. In contrast, our SCoT’s proactive detection of underlying harmful intent offers a promising direction for countering such complex jailbreak strategies.
越狱攻击。在第 2 节中，我们专注于单轮对话中的语言和自适应越狱攻击。然而，已经提出了许多其他越狱变体。一些工作旨在使越狱提示更加隐蔽[16]。另一些工作探索多轮越狱，特别是通过细粒度任务分解，其中恶意查询被分解成几个看似无害的子问题[44,59,60,38,26,2]。虽然这种策略通常能够绕过当前的安全机制，但通过将此类分解的恶意查询纳入安全训练数据可以缓解这种情况。相比之下，我们 SCoT 的主动检测潜在有害意图为应对此类复杂越狱策略提供了有前景的方向。

Brittleness of Safety Alignment. Recent research has revealed significant vulnerabilities in the safety mechanisms of large language models (LLMs)[35]. Beyond jailbreak attacks, studies show that modifying only a small subset of model parameters can compromise safety[46, 7, 58, 61]. Moreover, further fine-tuning LLMs—even on benign datasets—can paradoxically degrade safety behavior, highlighting the fragility of current alignment practices [52, 17, 35, 55, 51, 53, 36, 52]. These findings underscore the urgent need for a deeper understanding of safety mechanisms and the development of more robust and resilient defense strategies.
安全对齐的脆弱性。近期研究表明，大型语言模型（LLMs）的安全机制存在严重漏洞[ 35]。除了越狱攻击外，研究显示，仅修改模型参数的一小部分就可能危及安全[ 46, 7, 58, 61]。此外，进一步微调 LLMs——即使是在良性数据集上——也会反常地降低安全行为，这突显了当前对齐实践的脆弱性[ 52, 17, 35, 55, 51, 53, 36, 52]。这些发现强调了深入理解安全机制和开发更强大、更具弹性的防御策略的迫切需求。

Defense for LLMs safety. There are two primary lines of research in defending LLMs. The first is machine unlearning, which aims to erase harmful knowledge from the model entirely [57, 41, 63]. However, ensuring that all harmful behaviors have been completely forgotten is inherently difficult to verify. A second major direction is refusal training, which conditions models to reject harmful prompts [5, 30]. More recently, leveraging model reasoning capabilities has emerged as a promising direction to enhance LLM safety, particularly for sophisticated reasoning models such as DeepSeek-R1 [15] and QwQ [42]. An example is Deliberative Alignment[14], which aims to improve safety in proprietary, large-scale reasoning models (e.g., O1[20]). Despite such efforts, recent studies reveal that even these advanced reasoning models remain vulnerable to jailbreak attacks [22, 24]. This underscores the ongoing challenge of ensuring their intermediate reasoning chains consistently align with predefined safety objectives [62]. Our work distinctively targets general non-reasoning models that typically lack the advanced reasoning capabilities. We introduce a transparent, reproducible framework—leveraging adaptive question evolution, safety-oriented chain-of-thought construction, and curated dataset generation—to provide robust jailbreak defense for this critical, widely accessible LLM segment.
防御 LLMs 安全。防御 LLMs 的研究主要有两条主线。第一条是机器卸载，旨在从模型中完全清除有害知识[57, 41, 63]。然而，确保所有有害行为都被完全遗忘本质上很难验证。第二条主要方向是拒绝训练，它使模型能够拒绝有害提示[5, 30]。最近，利用模型推理能力已成为增强 LLMs 安全性的一个有前景的方向，特别是对于 DeepSeek-R1[15]和 QwQ[42]等复杂的推理模型。一个例子是 Deliberative Alignment[14]，它旨在提高专有的大规模推理模型（例如 O1[20]）的安全性。尽管付出了这些努力，最近的研究表明，即使这些先进的推理模型仍然容易受到 jailbreak 攻击[22, 24]。这突显了确保它们的中间推理链始终与预定义的安全目标保持一致这一持续挑战[62]。我们的工作独特地针对通常缺乏高级推理能力的通用非推理模型。 我们介绍了一个透明、可复现的框架，该框架利用自适应问题进化、安全导向的思维链构建和精选数据集生成，为这一关键且广泛可访问的 LLM 领域提供强大的越狱防御。

6 Conclusion and Future Work
6 结论与未来工作

In this paper, we investigate the vulnerability of large language models to the prominent jailbreak attacks. We critique that existing defense mechanisms fail to defeat the advanced attacks due to their inadequate training strategies. We propose SCoT, a novel approach that enhances LLMs by enabling them to assess user intent prior to generating responses. By expanding the training dataset with distractions and employing a reasoning-based safety chain, the safety-enhanced LLM can evaluate request intent against safety regulations. Experimental results demonstrate that SCoT outperforms existing defenses, effectively thwarting various jailbreak attempts and improving model resilience.
在这篇论文中，我们研究了大型语言模型对突出 jailbreak 攻击的脆弱性。我们批评现有的防御机制由于训练策略不足而无法击败高级攻击。我们提出了 SCoT，这是一种通过使 LLM 在生成响应之前能够评估用户意图来增强 LLM 的新方法。通过在训练数据集中添加干扰并采用基于推理的安全链，安全增强型 LLM 可以根据安全法规评估请求意图。实验结果表明，SCoT 优于现有防御机制，有效阻止了各种 jailbreak 尝试并提高了模型的鲁棒性。

Our SCoT has two limitations. First, it incurs slightly slower response times with additional computational overhead for safety reasoning when processing benign inputs—a natural trade-off between safety and practicality. Second, it relies on predefined safety regulations during training, limiting its adaptability to unseen scenarios. Future work could explore retrieving and reasoning over safety policies from external databases [25], enabling more fine-grained, dynamic safety reasoning and improving generalization to diverse safety-critical contexts.
我们的 SCoT 有两个局限性。首先，在处理良性输入时，由于安全推理需要额外的计算开销，响应时间会略微变慢——这是安全性和实用性之间的自然权衡。其次，它在训练时依赖于预定义的安全规则，这限制了它对未见过场景的适应性。未来的工作可以探索从外部数据库检索和推理安全策略[25]，实现更细粒度、动态的安全推理，并提高对多样化安全关键场景的泛化能力。
