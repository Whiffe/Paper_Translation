Reasoning-Augmented Conversation for Multi-Turn Jailbreak Attacks on Large Language Models
基于推理的对话增强多轮越狱攻击在大语言模型上

\fnmZonghao \surYing    \fnmDeyue \surZhang    \fnmZonglei \surJing    \fnmYisong \surXiao    \fnmQuanchen \surZou    \fnmAishan \surLiu    \fnmSiyuan \surLiang    \fnmXiangzheng \surZhang    \fnmXianglong \surLiu    \fnmDacheng \surTao [ [ [ [
Abstract  摘要

Multi-turn jailbreak attacks simulate real-world human interactions by engaging large language models (LLMs) in iterative dialogues, exposing critical safety vulnerabilities. However, existing methods often struggle to balance semantic coherence with attack effectiveness, resulting in either benign semantic drift or ineffective detection evasion. To address this challenge, we propose Reasoning-Augmented Conversation (RACE), a novel multi-turn jailbreak framework that reformulates harmful queries into benign reasoning tasks and leverages LLMs’ strong reasoning capabilities to compromise safety alignment. Specifically, we introduce an attack state machine framework to systematically model problem translation and iterative reasoning, ensuring coherent query generation across multiple turns. Building on this framework, we design gain-guided exploration, self-play, and rejection feedback modules to preserve attack semantics, enhance effectiveness, and sustain reasoning-driven attack progression. Extensive experiments on multiple LLMs demonstrate that RACE achieves state-of-the-art attack effectiveness in complex conversational scenarios, with attack success rates (ASRs) increasing by up to 96%. Notably, our approach achieves ASRs of 82% and 92% against leading commercial models, OpenAI o1 and DeepSeek R1, underscoring its potency. We release our code at https://github.com/NY1024/RACE to facilitate further research in this critical domain. Warning: This paper contains model outputs that are unsafe.
多轮越狱攻击通过让大型语言模型（LLMs）参与迭代对话来模拟现实世界的人类互动，从而暴露关键的安全漏洞。然而，现有方法往往难以在语义连贯性和攻击有效性之间取得平衡，导致要么出现良性语义漂移，要么无法有效规避检测。为了应对这一挑战，我们提出了推理增强对话（RACE），这是一个新颖的多轮越狱框架，它将有害查询重新表述为良性推理任务，并利用 LLMs 强大的推理能力来破坏安全对齐。具体而言，我们引入了一个攻击状态机框架，以系统性地建模问题转换和迭代推理，确保在多轮对话中生成连贯的查询。基于此框架，我们设计了增益引导探索、自我博弈和拒绝反馈模块，以保持攻击语义、增强有效性，并持续推动推理驱动的攻击进程。 在多个 LLMs 上的大量实验表明，RACE 在复杂对话场景中实现了最先进的攻击效果，攻击成功率（ASR）最高可提升 96%。值得注意的是，我们的方法对领先的商业模型 OpenAI o1 和 DeepSeek R1 的 ASR 分别达到了 82%和 92%，这突显了其强大效力。我们已将代码发布在 https://github.com/NY1024/RACE，以促进该关键领域的进一步研究。警告：本文包含不安全的模型输出。

1 Introduction  1 引言

LLMs have garnered widespread attention due to their remarkable ability to perform diverse tasks llm1 ; llm2 ; llm3 . However, studies have shown that LLMs can also generate unsafe or harmful content when prompted in certain ways recent1 ; recent2 ; recent3 . This vulnerability can be exploited through jailbreak attacks—carefully crafted prompts that bypass alignment constraints and elicit unintended responses gcg ; autodan ; nba ; dlp . Although harmful, jailbreak attacks gcg ; bap ; li2024semantic ; ying2024jailbreak ; zhang2024lanevil serve as a key red-teaming approach for assessing the risk of LLMs liang2024vl ; liang2024revisiting ; liang2023badclip generating unsafe content.
LLMs 因其卓越的多样化任务执行能力而备受关注；llm1；llm2；llm3。然而，研究表明，当以特定方式提示时，LLMs 也可能生成不安全或有害的内容 recent1；recent2；recent3。这种漏洞可以通过越狱攻击来利用——精心设计的提示绕过对齐约束并诱发出意外响应 gcg；autodan；nba；dlp。尽管有害，越狱攻击 gcg；bap；li2024semantic；ying2024jailbreak；zhang2024lanevil 作为评估 LLMs liang2024vl；liang2024revisiting；liang2023badclip 生成不安全内容风险的关键红队方法。

Currently, these attacks can be broadly categorized into single-turn and multi-turn jailbreaks. Single-turn attacks attempt to bypass safety mechanisms within a single interaction gcg ; autodan ; lapid2023open ; psy ; psy1 ; psy2 , whereas multi-turn jailbreaks exploit the interactive nature of LLMs by engaging them in iterative dialogues that lead to unsafe outputs mpsy2 ; mutliattack2 ; mpsy1 ; mutliattack5 . Compared to single-turn attacks, multi-turn jailbreaks simulate real-world human interactions and can expose critical safety blind spots, thereby attracting extensive interest mutliattack2 ; mutliattack5 . However, existing multi-turn jailbreak methods often struggle to maintain a balance between semantic coherence and attack effectiveness. In other words, they either cause benign semantic drift (where the conversation deviates from the original harmful objective) or fail to bypass alignment constraints, thereby limiting their overall attack performance.
目前，这些攻击可以大致分为单轮和多轮越狱。单轮攻击试图在单次交互中绕过安全机制 gcg; autodan; lapid2023open; psy; psy1; psy2，而多轮越狱则通过让 LLMs 参与迭代对话来利用其交互特性，最终导致不安全的输出 mpsy2; mutliattack2; mpsy1; mutliattack5。与单轮攻击相比，多轮越狱模拟了现实世界中的人类交互，能够暴露关键的安全盲点，因此吸引了广泛的关注 mutliattack2; mutliattack5。然而，现有的多轮越狱方法往往难以在语义连贯性和攻击有效性之间保持平衡。换句话说，它们要么导致良性语义漂移（即对话偏离了原始的恶意目标），要么无法绕过对齐约束，从而限制了它们的整体攻击性能。

To address this, we propose Reasoning-Augmented ConvErsation (RACE), a jailbreak framework that exploits LLMs’ strong reasoning capabilities reasonllm1 ; reasonllm2 by reformulating harmful queries into benign reasoning tasks. These benign and complex reasoning tasks are carefully designed such that their completion inherently leads the model to generate harmful content, effectively compromising safety alignment. To structure this process, we introduce an Attack State Machine (ASM) reasoning framework based on a finite state machine fsmbase2 ; fsmbase1 , which organizes jailbreaks into a sequence of reasoning states and transitions, ensuring semantic alignment and coherence. Building on this framework, we design gain-guided exploration, self-play, and rejection feedback modules to preserve attack semantics, enhance effectiveness, and sustain reasoning-driven attack progression. Specifically, gain-guided exploration selects queries that remain semantically aligned with the target while extracting useful information to ensure steady progress. Self-play simulates rejection responses within a shadow model, refining queries in advance and increasing success rates against the victim model. Rejection feedback adapts failed queries into alternative reasoning tasks, enabling rapid recovery and sustained attack stability. By combining these modules, RACE enables a structured and adaptive jailbreak method that is both highly effective yet challenging to mitigate. Fig. 1 illustrates the attack diagram of RACE.
为解决这一问题，我们提出了推理增强对话（RACE），这是一个利用 LLMs 强大推理能力 reasonllm1; reasonllm2 的越狱框架，通过将有害查询重新表述为良性推理任务。这些良性且复杂的推理任务经过精心设计，使其完成过程本质上会引导模型生成有害内容，从而有效破坏安全对齐。为构建这一流程，我们引入了一个基于有限状态机 fsmbase2; fsmbase1 的攻击状态机（ASM）推理框架，将越狱过程组织为一系列推理状态和转换，确保语义对齐和连贯性。在此框架基础上，我们设计了增益引导探索、自我博弈和拒绝反馈模块，以保持攻击语义、增强有效性并持续推动推理驱动的攻击进程。具体而言，增益引导探索选择在保持与目标语义对齐的同时提取有用信息的查询，以确保稳步推进。自我博弈在影子模型中模拟拒绝响应，提前优化查询并提高对目标模型的成功率。 拒绝反馈将失败的查询转化为替代性推理任务，实现快速恢复和持续的攻击稳定性。通过结合这些模块，RACE 能够以一种结构化和自适应的越狱方法，该方法既高效又难以缓解。图 1 展示了 RACE 的攻击示意图。

We conducted extensive experiments on multiple LLMs to evaluate the effectiveness of RACE in multi-turn jailbreak scenarios. The results demonstrate that RACE achieves attack success rates (ASRs) of up to 96%, highlighting its capability in complex conversational settings. Notably, our approach attained ASRs of 82% and 92% against the leading commercial models, OpenAI o1 and DeepSeek R1, respectively. These findings underscore the potency of reasoning-driven jailbreak attacks and the pressing need for stronger safety mechanisms. We hope our work will contribute to advancing LLM safety research and improving awareness of the potential misuse of LLMs’ reasoning capabilities.
我们在多个 LLMs 上进行了广泛的实验，以评估 RACE 在多轮越狱场景中的有效性。结果表明，RACE 实现了高达 96%的攻击成功率（ASR），突显了其在复杂对话环境中的能力。值得注意的是，我们的方法对领先的商业模型 OpenAI o1 和 DeepSeek R1 分别达到了 82%和 92%的 ASR。这些发现强调了推理驱动越狱攻击的效力，以及加强安全机制的紧迫性。我们希望我们的工作能够为 LLM 安全研究的发展做出贡献，并提高对 LLM 推理能力潜在滥用的认识。

2 Related work  2 相关工作

Reasoning in LLMs. Reasoning is a cognitive process that involves thinking about something logically and systematically, using evidence and past experiences to draw conclusions or make decisions reason1 ; reason2 . Recent studies have demonstrated that LLMs exhibit remarkable reasoning capabilities in various tasks, including mathematical reasoning reasonllm1 , common sense reasoning reasonllm2 , symbolic reasoning reasonllm3 , and causal reasoning reasonllm4 . Subsequently, Chain-of-thought (CoT) cot1 ; cot2 ; cot3 ; cot4 ; cot5 has emerged as a promising approach for further enhancing these reasoning capabilities.
LLMs 中的推理。推理是一种逻辑和系统性地思考的认知过程，利用证据和过去的经验得出结论或做出决策 reason1; reason2。最近的研究表明，LLMs 在各种任务中表现出卓越的推理能力，包括数学推理 reasonllm1、常识推理 reasonllm2、符号推理 reasonllm3 和因果推理 reasonllm4。随后，思维链（CoT）cot1; cot2; cot3; cot4; cot5 作为一种有前景的方法，进一步增强了这些推理能力。

While the reasoning capabilities of LLMs have contributed to their impressive performance across various downstream tasks, their potential exploitation in jailbreak attacks remains largely unexplored. In this study, we focus on leveraging reasoning capabilities to facilitate jailbreak attacks.
尽管 LLMs 的推理能力有助于它们在各种下游任务中取得令人印象深刻的性能，但它们在越狱攻击中的潜在利用仍 largely unexplored。在本研究中，我们专注于利用推理能力来促进越狱攻击。

Multi-turn Jailbreak Attack. Typical multi-turn jailbreak methods follow the principle of starting with harmless conversations and gradually making the queries more harmful in subsequent turns. Different methods have designed specific strategies based on this principle, including applying cognitive psychology theories to gradually modify subsequent queries mpsy1 ; mpsy2 , using actor networks to expand the attack range of subsequent queries ren2024 , extracting harmful keywords from original queries to construct semantically equivalent ones coa ; cfa , and breaking down the target query into multiple subqueries and merging the corresponding answers to achieve the final jailbreak sub1 ; sub2 .
多轮越狱攻击。典型的多轮越狱方法遵循从无害对话开始，在后续回合中逐渐使查询更具危害性的原则。不同的方法基于这一原则设计了特定的策略，包括应用认知心理学理论来逐渐修改后续查询 mpsy1; mpsy2，利用行动者网络来扩展后续查询的攻击范围 ren2024，从原始查询中提取有害关键词来构建语义等价的新查询 coa; cfa，以及将目标查询分解为多个子查询并将相应的答案合并以实现最终越狱 sub1; sub2。

Existing multi-turn jailbreak methods often suffer from semantic drift or fail to generate effective attacks. In contrast, our approach leverages LLMs’ reasoning capabilities to ensure a stable and effective jailbreak process.
现有的多轮越狱方法常常存在语义漂移或无法生成有效的攻击。相比之下，我们的方法利用 LLMs 的推理能力来确保稳定有效的越狱过程。

4 Methodology  4 方法

4.1 Motivation and Design Principle
4.1 动机与设计原则

LLMs have demonstrated strong reasoning capabilities in tasks such as logical deduction, common sense reasoning, and mathematical problem-solving, enabling them to tackle complex tasks across diverse domains reasonllm1 ; reasonllm2 ; reasonllm3 ; reasonllm4 . Rather than directly issuing harmful queries, which are easily rejected by safety alignment mechanisms, we propose a novel approach that exploits LLMs’ reasoning processes by reframing harmful intent into seemingly benign yet complex reasoning tasks. These tasks are carefully designed so that, once solved, they inherently guide the model to generate harmful content, effectively compromising its safety alignment. Here, the target LLM simultaneously acts as both the shadow model and the victim model. Independently, each role appears to engage in legitimate reasoning: the victim model focuses solely on solving reasoning tasks, while the shadow model refines and generates queries without explicitly recognizing the harmful intent behind them. However, when combined, these interactions ultimately lead to a successful attack.
LLMs 在逻辑推理、常识推理和数学问题解决等任务中展现出强大的推理能力，使它们能够处理跨不同领域复杂任务 reasonllm1; reasonllm2; reasonllm3; reasonllm4。我们提出了一种新颖的方法，通过将有害意图重新构建为看似无害但复杂的推理任务来利用 LLMs 的推理过程。这些任务经过精心设计，一旦解决，就会内在地引导模型生成有害内容，从而有效地破坏其安全对齐。在这里，目标 LLM 同时充当影子模型和受害者模型。独立地，每个角色都似乎在进行合法的推理：受害者模型仅专注于解决推理任务，而影子模型在不明确识别其背后的有害意图的情况下改进和生成查询。然而，当结合在一起时，这些交互最终导致了一次成功的攻击。

However, implementing this reasoning-driven jailbreak is non-trivial, as it requires manipulating the model’s reasoning process without triggering safety mechanisms. This poses three challenges: ❶ how to maintain reasoning alignment while ensuring that each query remains semantically consistent with the target and extracts useful information, ❷ how to preemptively optimize the query’s reasoning structure to avoid potential rejections during actual interactions, and ❸ how to quickly recover and learn from failed reasoning attempts to maintain attack progression. To address these challenges, we model the jailbreak process as an Attack State Machine (ASM), which serves as a reasoning planner. The ASM formalizes the attack as a structured sequence of reasoning states and transitions, ensuring that each step remains within the bounds of a legitimate problem-solving task while progressing toward the jailbreak objective. Within this reasoning framework, we implement three key modules to manipulate the model’s reasoning process and systematically address these challenges. ❶ The Gain-guided Exploration module selects queries that remain semantically aligned with the target while extracting useful information, ensuring steady attack progression. ❷ The Self-play module preemptively refines queries within the shadow model by simulating potential rejection responses, improving attack efficiency before engaging the victim model. ❸ The Rejection Feedback module analyzes failed interactions and restructures queries into alternative reasoning challenges, enabling quick recovery and maintaining attack stability. 4.2 Attack State Machine Framework

4.3 Attack Modules  4.3 攻击模块

Within the ASM, three specialized modules work together to optimize state transitions and ensure attack progression. The gain-guided exploration and self-play modules proactively generate and optimize effective queries, while the rejection feedback module handles failed state transitions by refining queries. The design enables the ASM to maintain stable progression through the reasoning states while efficiently adapting to model responses.
在 ASM 中，三个专门模块协同工作以优化状态转换并确保攻击进程。增益引导探索和自我博弈模块主动生成和优化有效查询，而拒绝反馈模块通过优化查询处理失败的状态转换。该设计使 ASM 能够在推理状态中保持稳定进展，同时高效适应模型响应。

4.4 Overall Attack  4.4 总体攻击

The attack begins by initializing the ASM reasoning states. In each turn, the shadow model generates seed queries that are refined through gain-guided exploration and self-play optimization. Successful queries advance the attack to the next state, while failed attempts trigger query refinement through the rejection feedback module. This process iterates until reaching the final state, maintaining a natural reasoning flow while pursuing the attack goal.
攻击开始时初始化 ASM 推理状态。每一轮中，影子模型生成种子查询，这些查询通过增益引导探索和自我博弈优化进行精炼。成功的查询将攻击推进到下一状态，而失败的尝试则通过拒绝反馈模块触发查询精炼。该过程迭代进行，直到达到最终状态，在追求攻击目标的同时保持自然的推理流程。

Currently, test-time defenses for multi-turn jailbreak attacks are lacking. While training-based approaches like dataset construction and fine-tuning improve robustness, they are unsuitable for test-time defenses. Thus, we evaluate popular single-turn defenses against RACE.

6.1 Number of Conversation Turns

The number of conversation turns serves as a crucial hyperparameter that significantly influences the effectiveness of multi-turn jailbreak attack. We evaluate its impact using ASR and HRI. As depicted in Fig. 4(b), we observe a systematic escalation in the harmfulness of model outputs as the number of conversation turns increases. This progression stems from two complementary mechanisms: initially, harmful content emerges from the inherent reasoning processes, where the victim model inadvertently exposes potentially unsafe information while attempting to solve complex queries; subsequently, the shadow model increasingly demands more intricate reasoning processes to incrementally extract increasingly detailed and potentially unsafe content. The results substantiate RACE’s ability to perform jailbreaks through systematic multi-turn interactions.
如图 4(b)所示，随着对话轮次数量的增加，模型输出的危害性呈现系统性上升。这一进程源于两个互补的机制：首先，有害内容源于固有的推理过程，在受害者模型试图解决复杂查询时，不自觉地暴露了潜在的不安全信息；其次，影子模型越来越多地要求更复杂的推理过程，以逐步提取越来越详细且潜在不安全的内容。结果证实了 RACE 通过系统性多轮交互进行越狱的能力。

We evaluate four types of reasoning tasks: mathematical reasoning (MaR), common sense reasoning (CoR), symbolic reasoning (SyR), and causal reasoning (CaR), whose definitions and examples are detailed in Sec. D.
我们评估了四种类型的推理任务：数学推理（MaR）、常识推理（CoR）、符号推理（SyR）和因果推理（CaR），其定义和示例在 D 节中详细说明。

6.3 Ablation on Attack Modules
6.3 攻击模块的消融实验

Fig. 6 presents the ablation study results for RACE. We analyze the performance impact when removing GE, SP, and RF.

Without GE, ASR drops by up to 14.0%, indicating the importance of selective query generation based on information gain. 7 Conclusion  7 结论

This paper presents a novel reasoning-driven jailbreak framework that exploits LLMs’ inherent reasoning capabilities to bypass built-in safety mechanisms. By modeling the attack process as an attack state machine, our approach strategically frames harmful intent as complex yet seemingly benign reasoning tasks, ensuring a structured and adaptive attack progression. We introduce three key modules, including gain-guided exploration, self-play, and rejection feedback to systematically manipulate the model’s reasoning process, optimize query structures, and recover from failed attempts. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method effectively compromises existing safety alignments, revealing critical risks to LLM safety.
本文提出了一种新颖的推理驱动越狱框架，该框架利用 LLMs 固有的推理能力来绕过内置的安全机制。通过将攻击过程建模为攻击状态机，我们的方法将有害意图策略性地构建为复杂但看似无害的推理任务，确保攻击过程的结构化和适应性。我们引入了三个关键模块，包括增益引导探索、自我博弈和拒绝反馈，以系统地操纵模型的推理过程、优化查询结构并从失败的尝试中恢复。大量实验表明，我们的方法有效地破坏了现有的安全对齐，揭示了 LLM 安全的关键风险。

8 Limitations  8 局限性

Despite the effectiveness of RACE, several challenges remain to be addressed: ❶ improving efficiency to minimize interaction overhead while maintaining high ASRs, ❷ developing adaptive countermeasures to mitigate reasoning-based attacks, and ❸ extending the framework to analyze and defend against other forms of adversarial reasoning manipulations wang2025black ; zhang2024visual .
尽管 RACE 效果显著，但仍存在一些挑战需要解决：❶提高效率以最小化交互开销，同时保持高 ASR；❷开发自适应的对抗措施以减轻基于推理的攻击；❸将框架扩展到分析和防御其他形式的对抗性推理操纵，如 wang2025black 和 zhang2024visual 所述。

This work demonstrates that current alignment strategies may be insufficient in preventing multi-turn jailbreaks, particularly when exploiting reasoning capabilities. We strongly oppose any malicious applications of our findings and have included discussions on potential countermeasures. While the development of comprehensive defense mechanisms liu2023exploring ; liang2024unlearning remains future work, we believe this research provides valuable insights for LLM developers to develop more robust alignment techniques.
