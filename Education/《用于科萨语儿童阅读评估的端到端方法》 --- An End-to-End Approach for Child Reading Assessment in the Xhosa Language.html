An End-to-End Approach for Child Reading Assessment in the Xhosa Language
《用于科萨语儿童阅读评估的端到端方法》

Sergio Chevtchenko 1122    Nikhil Navas 1122    Rafaella Vale 44    Franco Ubaudi 55    Sipumelele Lucwaba 66    Cally Ardington 33    Soheil Afshar 77    Mark Antoniou 22    Saeed Afshar 1122
Abstract  摘要

Child literacy is a strong predictor of life outcomes at the subsequent stages of an individual’s life. This points to a need for targeted interventions in vulnerable low and middle income populations to help bridge the gap between literacy levels in these regions and high income ones. In this effort, reading assessments provide an important tool to measure the effectiveness of these programs and AI can be a reliable and economical tool to support educators with this task. Developing accurate automatic reading assessment systems for child speech in low-resource languages poses significant challenges due to limited data and the unique acoustic properties of children’s voices. This study focuses on Xhosa, a language spoken in South Africa, to advance child speech recognition capabilities. We present a novel dataset composed of child speech samples in Xhosa. The dataset is available upon request and contains ten words and letters, which are part of the Early Grade Reading Assessment (EGRA) system. Each recording is labeled with an online and cost-effective approach by multiple markers and a subsample is validated by an independent EGRA reviewer. This dataset is evaluated with three fine-tuned state-of-the-art end-to-end models: wav2vec 2.0, HuBERT, and Whisper. The results indicate that the performance of these models can be significantly influenced by the amount and balancing of the available training data, which is fundamental for cost-effective large dataset collection. Furthermore, our experiments indicate that the wav2vec 2.0 performance is improved by training on multiple classes at a time, even when the number of available samples is constrained.
儿童识字能力是个人在人生后续阶段生活结果的重要预测指标。这表明需要针对弱势的低收入和中等收入人群采取有针对性的干预措施，以帮助缩小这些地区与高收入地区在识字水平上的差距。在这一努力中，阅读评估为衡量这些项目效果提供了重要工具，而人工智能则可以成为支持教育工作者完成这项任务的可靠且经济的工具。为低资源语言中的儿童语音开发准确的自动阅读评估系统面临着由于数据有限和儿童语音独特的声学特性所带来的重大挑战。本研究聚焦于南非使用的科萨语，以提升儿童语音识别能力。我们提出一个由科萨语儿童语音样本组成的新型数据集。该数据集可应要求提供，其中包含十种单词和字母，这些是早期年级阅读评估（EGRA）系统的一部分。每段录音都通过多位标记者的在线且经济高效的方法进行标注，并且一个子样本由独立的 EGRA 审阅者进行验证。 该数据集使用三种微调的端到端模型进行评估：wav2vec 2.0、HuBERT 和 Whisper。结果表明，这些模型的性能会受到可用训练数据数量和平衡性的显著影响，这对于经济高效的大数据集收集至关重要。此外，我们的实验表明，即使可用样本数量受限，通过同时训练多个类别也能提升 wav2vec 2.0 的性能。

1 Introduction  1 引言

Reading assessment in the early grades is essential for pinpointing the difficulties children encounter while learning to read. By identifying these challenges, educators can design targeted interventions that accelerate literacy development and foster inclusion. Artificial intelligence offers a practical way to scale such assessments, providing rapid insights into children’s oral-reading skills and the obstacles revealed by pronunciation tasks, even when data and resources are limited.
低年级的阅读评估对于识别儿童在学习阅读过程中遇到的困难至关重要。通过识别这些挑战，教育者可以设计有针对性的干预措施，加速读写能力发展并促进包容性。人工智能提供了一种实用的方法来扩展此类评估，即使在数据和资源有限的情况下，也能快速洞察儿童的朗读能力和发音任务揭示的障碍。

The motivation for this research is a pragmatic one. Good education is a crucial component for facilitating a favorable future for children. While assessing speech fluency and reading skills is a valid way to inform about the advancement of literacy acquisition in children [9], a simple approach to evaluating their aptitude to understand text in early years is to analyze their ability to correctly pronounce words [16], a key focus of this research. A poor ability to pronounce words is indicative of possible issues with hearing, understanding grammar, or other difficulties such as dyslexia [25]. As a result, uncovering these issues quickly can promote focused guidance from an early age that greatly improves a child’s educational outcome [13, 25].
这项研究的动机是务实的。良好的教育是促进儿童美好未来的关键组成部分。虽然评估口语流利度和阅读能力是了解儿童识字能力进步的有效方式[9]，但评估他们早期理解文本能力的一种简单方法就是分析他们正确发音单词的能力[16]，这也是本研究的主要关注点。发音能力差可能表明存在听力问题、语法理解困难或其他如阅读障碍[25]等问题。因此，快速发现这些问题可以促进早期针对性指导，从而显著提高儿童的教育成果[13,25]。

To tackle this, the use of automatic speech recognition (ASR) can greatly assist in finding reading issues that manifest themselves in pronunciation difficulties. ASR can help automate thorough and accurate assessment of a child’s ability and the determination of progress as educators seek to address problems. However, the effectiveness of ASR can be greatly impacted in the context of low-resource languages (LRL) [14, 21], that is, languages with limited availability of resources, for the effective automated construction of models needed for assessing children’s speech. Hence this research also focuses on languages that are characterized as LRL, an example of which is the South African language Xhosa, which also provides phonetic complexity challenges to this research [15].
为解决这一问题，自动语音识别（ASR）技术可以在发现发音困难所体现的阅读问题方面提供极大帮助。ASR 有助于自动化评估儿童的能力，并帮助教育工作者确定进步情况，从而解决这些问题。然而，在低资源语言（LRL）的背景下，ASR 的有效性可能受到极大影响[14, 21]，即资源有限的语言，这些语言缺乏构建用于评估儿童语音所需的有效自动模型资源。因此，这项研究也关注被归类为 LRL 的语言，例如南非语言科萨语，它也为这项研究提供了语音复杂性挑战[15]。

This research investigates the use of state-of-the-art (SOTA) speech classification models that convert the audio of child speech into the phonetics responsible for the language of choice. The South African language Xhosa is used as the target language. Our investigation is principally focused on testing a cost-effective data-labeling method and understanding the impact of choices in model parameters, training set sizes, and model building strategies. The main goal of this research is the development of a solution that fulfills the following criteria: accurate assessment of a child’s reading ability; coping with restrictions in terms of the amount of available training data; potential integration with the Early Grade Reading Assessment (EGRA) program [9], and providing a solution architecture that is easy to reuse for other speaking languages.
这项研究探讨了最先进的（SOTA）语音分类模型在将儿童语音音频转换为所选语言所负责的音素方面的应用。南非语言科萨语被用作目标语言。我们的研究主要集中于测试一种经济高效的数据标注方法，并理解模型参数选择、训练集大小和模型构建策略的影响。这项研究的主要目标是开发一种满足以下标准的解决方案：准确评估儿童的阅读能力；应对可用训练数据的限制；有潜力与早期年级阅读评估（EGRA）项目[9]集成，并提供一个易于为其他语言重用的解决方案架构。

Accurately assessing a child’s ability to pronounce words is time-consuming and prone to error, since it can be impacted by the ability and consistency of individual human assessors. As such, automation provides a way for avoiding these issues, while also reducing needed time and effort. Additionally, AI-powered assessments provide a means for consistently tracking a child’s progress, while reducing the impact of confounding variables such as human assessor subjectivity and enforcing the use of an identical assessment across time and individual children.
准确评估儿童拼读单词的能力既耗时又容易出错，因为其会受到个体评估者能力和一致性的影响。因此，自动化提供了一种避免这些问题的方式，同时减少了所需的时间和精力。此外，人工智能驱动的评估提供了一种持续跟踪儿童进步的方法，同时减少了人类评估者主观性等混杂变量的影响，并确保在时间和不同儿童之间使用相同的评估标准。

The next problem to address is regarding LRLs. Many speaking languages are characterized as LRLs because of a lack of large text corpora or other linguistic resources that are suitable for training ASR solutions. This presents a serious barrier since it impacts solution effectiveness and greatly increases the needed effort for developing ASR solutions. Novel approaches are needed for facilitating the development of accurate models, coping with limited resources, and managing variability of human expertise across limited staff. Given these challenges, the use of self-supervised SOTA speech recognition architectures offers a promising solution by reducing the reliance on extensive labeled datasets and enabling a more detailed focus on nuanced parts of speech.
接下来要解决的问题涉及低资源语言（LRLs）。许多口语语言被归类为 LRLs，因为缺乏适合训练语音识别（ASR）解决方案的大规模文本语料库或其他语言资源。这构成一个严重障碍，因为它影响解决方案的有效性，并大大增加了开发 ASR 解决方案所需的努力。需要创新方法来促进准确模型的开发，应对有限的资源，并管理有限员工中人类专业知识的差异性。鉴于这些挑战，使用自监督的顶尖（SOTA）语音识别架构提供了一种有前景的解决方案，通过减少对大量标记数据集的依赖，并使注意力能够更详细地集中于语音的细微部分。

The present work makes the following contributions to automated reading assessment for LRL. First, it introduces a new, high-quality dataset of Xhosa child reading samples, specifically designed for early grade reading assessment. This dataset has been made available to the community [6]. Second, it details and validates a cost-effective online method for data labeling and verification, demonstrating its suitability for resource-constrained settings. Third, it provides a comprehensive evaluation of three fine-tuned SOTA speech classification architectures on this dataset, highlighting how variations in training set size and class balancing can significantly impact performance.
当前工作为低资源语言的自动阅读评估做出了以下贡献。首先，它引入了一个新的、高质量的祖鲁语儿童阅读样本数据集，专门为早期年级阅读评估设计。该数据集已向社区开放[6]。其次，它详细介绍了验证数据标注和验证的低成本在线方法，并展示了其在资源受限环境中的适用性。第三，它对该数据集上三种微调的 SOTA 语音分类架构进行了全面评估，突出了训练集大小和类别平衡变化如何显著影响性能。

2 Related Work  2 相关工作

Among self-supervised models, wav2vec has emerged as a state-of-the-art architecture for ASR, using self-supervised learning to extract meaningful features from raw audio and requiring minimal labeled data for fine-tuning [3]. Doumbouya et al. [8] adapted wav2vec to the West African Radio Corpus, demonstrating sensitivity to features of African languages and effective identification in noisy multilingual settings. Similarly, our work involves fine-tuning a pre-trained wav2vec 2.0 model, augmented with a classifier multilayer perceptron to classify audio recordings of early grade children learning the Xhosa language as correct or incorrect pronunciations.
在自监督模型中，wav2vec 已成为语音识别领域的一种先进架构，它利用自监督学习从原始音频中提取有意义特征，并且只需少量标记数据进行微调[ 3]。Doumbouya 等人[ 8]将 wav2vec 应用于西非广播语料库，展示了其对非洲语言特征的敏感性，并在嘈杂的多语言环境中实现了有效识别。类似地，我们的工作涉及微调一个预训练的 wav2vec 2.0 模型，并增加一个分类多层感知器，用于将学习科萨语的低年级儿童音频录音分类为正确或错误的发音。

Wav2vec has also been effective in detecting pronunciation errors, offering potential in language learning and pronunciation assessment [18]. Shekar et al. [23] used wav2vec to analyze speech recordings of non-native speakers, detecting mispronunciations and capturing fine-grained phonetic details for phoneme-level error diagnosis. Recent research by Jain et al. [12] examined the use of wav2vec models in pediatric learning assessment, a challenging domain due to varied speech patterns in children. Abaskohi et al. [1] further developed and adapted wav2vec with a preprocessing technique called random frequency pitch, useful for capturing the frequency characteristics of children’s speech. Applied to Persian children’s speech and reading recordings, the model significantly reduced word error rates compared to baselines, confirming its effectiveness in pediatric assessment tasks. Obiang et al. [17] fine-tuned wav2vec for Yoruba, a low-resourced tonal language, effectively capturing complex pitch and pronunciation variations superior to traditional methods. Similarly, Okwugbe et al. [7] developed a deep learning model combining rCNNs and BiLSTMs with CTC for speech recognition of the Fon and Igbo languages, addressing challenges due to data scarcity.
Wav2vec 在检测发音错误方面也表现出效，为语言学习和发音评估提供了潜力[ 18]。Shekar 等人[ 23]使用 wav2vec 分析非母语者的语音录音，检测发音错误并捕捉精细的音素细节，用于音素级别的错误诊断。Jain 等人最近的[ 12]研究考察了 wav2vec 模型在儿童学习评估中的应用，这是一个具有挑战性的领域，因为儿童的语言模式各不相同。Abaskohi 等人[ 1]进一步开发和改进了 wav2vec，并引入了一种名为随机频率音调的预处理技术，这对于捕捉儿童语音的频率特征很有用。将该模型应用于波斯儿童的语音和阅读录音，与基线相比，该模型显著降低了单词错误率，证实了其在儿童评估任务中的有效性。Obiang 等人[ 17]针对低资源声调语言约鲁巴语微调了 wav2vec，有效地捕捉了比传统方法更复杂的音调发音变化。 类似地，Okwugbe 等人[7]开发了一个结合 rCNNs 和 BiLSTMs 以及 CTC 的深度学习模型，用于 Fon 和 Igbo 语言的语音识别，解决了由于数据稀缺带来的挑战。

Other works have tackled the application of ASR in assessing and improving reading skills, particularly in children. Sabu and Rao [22] sought to evaluate word reading accuracy and identify prosodic elements like phrase breaks and emphasized words in children studying English as a second language. The complexities of the prosodic events were reflected in the lower precision estimation shown by the system, revealing the importance of an accurate feature extraction. Shivakumar and Georgiou [24] used transfer learning from adult ASR models as a way to circumvent the lack of extensive availability of children speech data. Strategies to adapt to varying amounts of data and children’s ages were also recommended. Bai et al. [5] explored the evaluation of decoding skills in Dutch children to provide feedback meant to impact reading accuracy and speed. The study was further expanded in [4] to also highlight the most problematic words encountered by the students. Bachiri et al. [2] proposed an interactive learning system as a Moodle plugin to aid in child reading assessment in underserved educational settings. The authors make a case for the integration of AI-based speech recognition to enhance literacy development due to the positive results observed. In an attempt to investigate how AI performs in assessing students reading fluency and level compared to human experts, Yıldız et al. [26] found that AI offered reliable predictions that could contribute to lowering costs and improving efficiency.
其他研究探讨了语音识别技术在评估和提升阅读能力方面的应用，尤其是在儿童中。Sabu 和 Rao [22]试图评估儿童作为第二语言学习英语时的单词阅读准确度，并识别韵律元素，如短语断句和强调单词。韵律事件的复杂性体现在系统显示的较低精确度估计中，揭示了准确特征提取的重要性。Shivakumar 和 Georgiou [24]使用成人语音识别模型的迁移学习作为绕过儿童语音数据缺乏广泛可用性的方法。还建议了适应不同数据量和儿童年龄的策略。Bai 等人 [5]探索了荷兰儿童解码能力的评估，以提供旨在影响阅读准确度和速度的反馈。该研究在[4]中进一步扩展，以突出学生遇到的最有问题的单词。Bachiri 等人 [2]提出了一种交互式学习系统，作为 Moodle 插件，用于在教育资源不足的环境中辅助儿童阅读评估。 作者们基于观察到的积极结果，主张将基于 AI 的语音识别技术整合到阅读能力发展中。为了调查 AI 在评估学生阅读流利度和水平方面与人类专家的表现差异，Yıldız 等人[26]发现 AI 提供了可靠的预测，这有助于降低成本和提高效率。

The recent publication by Henkel et al. [10] has a significant overlap with the intended goals of the current work. The automation of reading assessment for students in a resource-constrained context is investigated to compare the potential of Whisper V2 and wav2vec 2.0 models against human raters. The models produce transcriptions of audio recordings of Ghanaian students reading to provide an estimate of the students’ oral reading fluency. The fluency scores given by Whisper V2 were found to be highly correlated with assessments by human experts, even without fine-tuning. It is important to note that the way these models were used to score reading fluency in students would not be suitable for the goal of the current work, since the lack of fine-tuning may result in overcorrections that ignore mispronunciations or in the misinterpretation of accents, as demonstrated in Section 4.1. As the present work aims to evaluate the pronunciation of words and letters by children in the Xhosa language, fine-tuning of the models is necessary to classify samples as correct or incorrect according to the target language.
Henkel 等人最近的发表[10]与当前工作的预期目标有显著重叠。在资源受限的环境中对学生的阅读评估进行自动化研究，以比较 Whisper V2 和 wav2vec 2.0 模型与人类评分员的潜力。这些模型生成加纳学生朗读音频录音的转录文本，以提供学生口语阅读流利度的估计。研究发现，Whisper V2 给出的流利度评分与人类专家的评估高度相关，即使没有进行微调。需要注意的是，这些模型用于评分学生阅读流利度的方式不适用于当前工作的目标，因为缺乏微调可能导致过度纠正，从而忽略发音错误或误读口音，正如第 4.1 节所示。由于当前工作旨在评估儿童在科萨语中单词和字母的发音，因此需要对模型进行微调，以便根据目标语言将样本分类为正确或错误。

The above studies underscore the effectiveness of speech-to-text models for low-resource languages and child speech recognition. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work to evaluate end-to-end models on words and letters for a child reading assessment problem.
上述研究强调了语音转文本模型在低资源语言和儿童语音识别方面的有效性。据我们所知，这是首次针对儿童阅读评估问题，在单词和字母层面上评估端到端模型的工作。

3 Methodology  3 方法

3.1 Dataset Description  3.1 数据集描述

The dataset was collected in 2024 as part of the Early Grade Reading Assessment (EGRA), conducted in grades 1 to 4 at schools in South Africa, and is available online [6]. This study focuses on the following ten letters and words in the Xhosa language: d, v, n, ewe, hayi, hl, inja, kude, molo and ng. A question is presented to the student, asking to pronounce one of these words or letters, followed by a recording. The recording stops when the student presses a button for the next question. The average duration of the recordings is around 4 seconds, with added variability mostly due to multiple attempts at pronouncing the same question.
该数据集于 2024 年收集，作为南非 1 至 4 年级学校早期阅读评估（EGRA）的一部分，并在线提供[6]。本研究关注以下十个科萨语字母和单词：d、v、n、ewe、hayi、hl、inja、kude、molo 和 ng。向学生提出一个问题，要求他们发音这些单词或字母中的一个，然后进行录音。当学生按下按钮进入下一个问题时，录音停止。录音的平均时长约为 4 秒，额外的变化主要由于多次尝试发音同一个问题。

A total of 14,971 recordings of the EGRA collection with over 19 hours in recording time collected over 8 months are made available upon request. Each recording is labeled as correct or incorrect by three fluent Xhosa speakers, resulting in a dataset of 44,913 labeled entries. Since some recordings received conflicting labels, a validation experiment was conducted, as described in Section 3.2. Figure 1 illustrates the distribution of these labels by question, showing that certain items can be more challenging to classify. For example, most children correctly pronounced the letter n, whereas a considerable number struggled with the consonant hl. To enable cost-effective and efficient data collection and labeling, custom mobile and server applications were developed as part of the project. A more detailed description of these tools will be provided in a forthcoming publication.

3.2 Data Labeling and Validation
3.2 数据标注与验证

Selecting appropriate validation criteria for training data is another significant challenge. To ensure high-quality recordings, each sample is labeled by three independent markers, and only those with consensus are included in training. To validate this method, 400 recordings were randomly selected from ten words in the dataset, with each word contributing 40 samples. These were evenly distributed across four marking scenarios: (a) all three markers labeled it as correct, (b) all three labeled it as incorrect, (c) one marked it incorrect while the other two marked it correct, and (d) one marked it correct while the other two marked it incorrect. An expert in traditional EGRA marking for Xhosa language then assessed these samples, and Table 1 presents the agreement rate between the expert and the original markers. An agreement rate per question is also presented in Figure 3. Thus, consensus-based approach retains approximately 85% of the original dataset, while maximizing data reliability. Consequently, while the provided dataset includes all 14,971 recordings, the experimental results in Section 4 are based only on the 12,747 consensus-marked recordings.
选择合适的验证标准来训练数据是另一个重大挑战。为确保高质量录音，每个样本由三个独立标记者标记，只有达成共识的样本才被纳入训练。为验证此方法，从数据集中的十个词中随机选取了 400 个录音，每个词贡献 40 个样本。这些样本均匀分布在四种标记场景中：(a)所有三个标记者都将其标记为正确，(b)所有三个标记者都将其标记为错误，(c)一个标记者标记为错误而另外两个标记为正确，(d)一个标记者标记为正确而另外两个标记为错误。然后由一位精通南非传统 EGRA 标记的专家评估这些样本，表 1 展示了专家与原始标记者之间的协议率。每个问题的协议率也显示在图 3 中。因此，基于共识的方法保留了原始数据集的大约 85%，同时最大限度地提高了数据可靠性。因此，尽管提供的数据集包含所有 14,971 个录音，但第 4 节的实验结果仅基于 12,747 个共识标记的录音。

5 Conclusion  5 结论

This paper presents a cost-effective method for collecting and labeling child speech data in a low-resource language, and for training an AI model to assess reading proficiency, tailored for early-grade learners. We introduce and make available for the community a challenging dataset of Xhosa child speech [6], recorded under realistic classroom conditions that include noise and multiple pronunciation attempts. The dataset contains 14,971 audio recordings, each containing a single word or letter, which are part of the Early Grade Reading Assessment (EGRA) system. The recordings were labeled online by three independent markers, and a sample was validated by an expert EGRA reviewer to confirm reliability.
本文提出了一种低成本的方法，用于收集和标注低资源语言中的儿童语音数据，并训练一个 AI 模型来评估阅读能力，专为低年级学习者设计。我们向社区介绍并提供了一个具有挑战性的科萨语儿童语音数据集[6]，该数据集在真实的课堂条件下录制，包括噪音和多次发音尝试。该数据集包含 14,971 个音频录音，每个录音包含一个单词或字母，这些录音是早期阅读评估（EGRA）系统的一部分。录音由三位独立标注员在线标注，并抽取样本由 EGRA 专家评审员验证以确保可靠性。

Three state-of-the-art speech recognition architectures were fine-tuned as binary classifiers and the experimental results indicate that data balancing and multi-question training strategies substantially affect performance. In particular, wav2vec 2.0 and Whisper are generally found to outperform HuBERT, achieving lower false positive and false negative rates. Although wav2vec 2.0 and Whisper demonstrate comparable accuracy, wav2vec 2.0 demands fewer computational resources during the training phase. Further comparisons with a large-scale multilingual ASR model (MMS-1B) confirm the effectiveness of the proposed approach, especially for short, noisy speech segments in a classroom setting.
三种最先进的语音识别架构被微调为二元分类器，实验结果表明数据平衡和多问题训练策略对性能有显著影响。特别是，wav2vec 2.0 和 Whisper 通常被认为优于 HuBERT，实现了更低的误报率和漏报率。尽管 wav2vec 2.0 和 Whisper 表现出相当的准确率，但在训练阶段 wav2vec 2.0 所需的计算资源更少。与大规模多语言语音识别模型（MMS-1B）的进一步比较证实了所提出方法的有效性，尤其是在教室环境中短而嘈杂的语音片段。

Future work is planned to focus on further improving the sample efficiency of the models, while providing a phoneme-based feedback to the user. Preliminary experiments on another LRL dataset, Sepedi, indicate similar performance for wav2vec 2.0 and this additional dataset is expected to be released in forthcoming research. Ultimately, the goal of this project is to develop a cost-effective reading tutoring system for early-grade learners, thereby supporting the preservation and educational advancement of low-resource languages.
未来的工作计划将着重于进一步提升模型的样本效率，同时为用户提供基于音素的反馈。在另一个 LRL 数据集——塞皮迪语上的初步实验表明，wav2vec 2.0 表现出相似的性能，这个额外的数据集预计将在未来的研究中发布。最终，该项目的目标是开发一个经济高效的阅读辅导系统，以支持低资源语言的保存和教育发展。
